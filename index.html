Research interests

For my master thesis (2013), I studied the static quark-antiquark potential in the pure gauge SU(3) theory. The technical aspects of this work consisted of modifying an existing Monte Carlo code and writing the measurements packages. For this project, I was running the simulations on the INFN cluster at the University of Rome "Sapienza".

In 2014, I moved to Trinity College Dublin (TCD) where I started working towards the PhD degree. My project deals with the non-perturbative renormalization of QCD parameters. The first part of the project consisted of defining the couplings in terms of gradient-flow dependent observables and performing a perturbative study of the cutoff effects relative to these observables. I am currently performing non-perturbative tests on such cutoff effects, using the open QCD code and extending it with a new measurement module. I run the simulations on the High Performance Computing cluster of TCD.

As far as my future perspectives are concerned, I believe the most interesting aspects of the physics beyond the Standard Model are flavor physics, the nature of the Higgs particle and the possible inclusion of the gravitational interaction eventually. The techniques I use and the skills I acquired have a number of applications which can be studied. Also, the inclusion of isospin-breaking effects (they are typically a few percent effects) is needed, due to the increasing precision reached in lattice simulations. More generally, I am interested in the whole realm of study concerning the challenging topics of QED and QCD by means of lattice explorations.
